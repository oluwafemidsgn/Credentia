## 2. What we collect and why

### 2a. When you search or browse the site

Our hosting and analytics infrastructure collects standard, anonymised data about how the site is used. This includes:

- Pages visited and checklists viewed
- Search terms entered
- Time spent on pages
- Approximate location (city or state level — not your precise address)
- Browser type and device type
- Referring website (how you arrived at Credentia)

**Why:** This helps us understand which checklists are most needed, which searches return no results (telling us what to build next), and how to improve the experience for everyone.

**Legal basis:** Legitimate interests — understanding usage helps us serve you better and keep the directory accurate.

### 2b. When you flag an issue

If you use the "Flag an issue" feature on a checklist, we collect:

- The specific correction or concern you submit
- The checklist or entry it relates to
- Your name and email address, if you choose to provide them for follow-up

**Why:** To review and action corrections to checklist entries. This is the feedback loop that keeps the directory accurate.

**Legal basis:** Legitimate interests — processing flags is how we maintain the accuracy you rely on.

Your name and email are optional. You can submit a flag without them.

### 2c. When you contact us

If you send us a message through the contact page, we collect your name, email address, and the contents of your message.

**Why:** To respond to your enquiry.

**Legal basis:** Legitimate interests — responding to messages you've sent us.

### 2d. If you support us financially

If you make a voluntary contribution through our support page, your payment is handled by a third-party payment processor (Paystack or a similar service). We receive confirmation that a contribution was made and the amount. We do not store your card details — those remain with the payment processor under their own privacy policy.

**Why:** To record the contribution for accounting purposes.

**Legal basis:** Contractual necessity — processing the transaction you initiated.

## 3. Cookies and third-party services

We use a small number of third-party services to operate the site. Some of these set cookies on your device.

| Service | What it does | Cookie type |
|---|---|---|
| **Analytics** (Google Analytics or Vercel Analytics) | Tracks aggregate, anonymised site usage | Analytics cookie |
| **Google AdSense** | Displays advertising to support the site | Advertising cookie — personalised |
| **Supabase** | Stores flag submissions and backend data | Functional (server-side only) |
| **Sanity** | Manages our content (checklists, blog posts) | No user-facing cookies |

**On advertising cookies:** If we display Google AdSense ads, Google may use cookies to show you relevant ads based on your browsing history across the web. You can manage this at [google.com/settings/ads](https://google.com/settings/ads) or opt out entirely using the [Google opt-out plugin](https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout).

**On analytics cookies:** You can prevent analytics tracking by enabling "Do Not Track" in your browser, using a browser extension like uBlock Origin, or adjusting your cookie preferences if we have a cookie consent banner.

We do not use cookies for any purpose beyond what the services above require to function.

## 4. Who we share your data with

We share data only with the third-party services listed in Section 3, and only to the extent necessary for those services to operate. Specifically:

- **Google:** Analytics and advertising data

**We do not:**

- Share your data with agents, advertisers (beyond Google's standard mechanisms), or marketing lists
- Transfer your data outside of Nigeria except where the services above require it (e.g. Google and Supabase infrastructure may be hosted outside Nigeria — we only use providers that comply with applicable data protection standards)

## 5. Your rights under the NDPR and Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023

Under Nigerian data protection law, you have the right to:

- **Access:** Know what personal data we hold about you
- **Correction:** Request that inaccurate data is corrected
- **Deletion:** Request that your personal data is deleted (the "right to be forgotten")
- **Objection:** Object to processing based on legitimate interests
- **Restriction:** Ask us to limit how we use your data in certain circumstances
- **Portability:** Receive your data in a portable format where technically feasible
- **Lodge a complaint:** With the **Nigeria Data Protection Commission (NDPC)** at [ndpc.gov.ng](https://ndpc.gov.ng) if you believe your rights have been violated

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at **[contact email]**. We will respond within 30 days.
